# Exercise around ovulation — moving with your energy

Around ovulation many people notice there's naturally a bit more energy. You might feel a
little more active, social or stronger than on other days of your cycle. It doesn't have
to be that way — every body is different and every month can feel different. But if that
energy is there, you can choose to use it in a pleasant way. In this article we describe
what can come up around this phase and how movement can gently fit in. No rules, no
musts — just room to listen to what your body enjoys today.

## Why do you often feel more energy around ovulation?

In the days around ovulation the hormonal balance in your body shifts. For many people
that goes together with a feeling of more drive, a clearer head or a desire to be active.
That's not an obligation to push harder — it's information. If you notice you feel
energetic, you may act on it. Feeling calmer instead? That's completely okay too. Your
body is not a machine that performs identically every month, and it doesn't have to be.

## What movement suits this phase?

For those who feel like more intensity, this phase can be a pleasant moment for forms of
movement that ask a bit more of you. Think of a brisk walk, dancing, a group class,
strength training or a run — whatever gives you joy. It's not about performing or proving
yourself, but about moving because it feels good. Maybe you enjoy moving together, since
many people feel a little more social around this time. Listen to what appeals to you and
let that lead.

## How do you listen to your body while exercising?

More energy doesn't mean you have to go past your limits. A good gauge is how you feel
during and after moving: do you feel refreshed and content, or drained and irritable?
Movement is allowed to give energy, not cost it. Rest when you need to, drink enough
water and allow yourself the moment to recover. There is no right or wrong way to feel
today — and rest is just as valuable as action.

## What if you don't feel energetic around ovulation?

Not everyone experiences the same wave of energy, and that's completely normal. Sleep,
stress, busyness or simply the day itself all play a role. Feeling calmer? Then gentle
yin yoga, a short stretch or a relaxed walk might be exactly enough. You don't have to
force your energy to "keep up" with your phase.
