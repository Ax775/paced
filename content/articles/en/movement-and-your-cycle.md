# Movement in tune with your cycle

Your energy isn't the same every day, and that's completely normal. Through your cycle,
all kinds of things shift in your body, and you may feel that in how you want to move.
One week a brisk walk feels like pure joy; the next, you mostly long for gentleness.
Tuning movement to your cycle doesn't mean following a strict schedule — it's an
invitation to listen to what suits you today. Below we describe what often happens per
phase, so you can choose for yourself.

## Why does my energy change through my cycle?

Through your cycle your hormones fluctuate, and those shifts can affect your energy,
your mood and your appetite for movement. Some days you feel naturally a bit stronger,
other days softer or more tired. That's not a sign something is wrong — it's part of
your body's rhythm. Movement is allowed to move with that rhythm rather than against it.
That way it becomes something that nourishes you, not something you force.

## How can I move during my period?

During your period your body works hard, and it's perfectly okay to take it easier.
Maybe a gentle walk, some light stretches or a calm yoga flow feels best today. Feeling
energetic? Then do what feels good. There is no right or wrong way. Rest is just as
valuable as movement, and you don't have to make up for anything. Read more about
[exercise during your period](/articles/exercise-during-your-period/).

## What suits the follicular and ovulatory phases?

In the follicular phase, after your period, many people notice a little more energy and
a desire to be active. Around ovulation that feeling sometimes peaks. This can be a nice
moment to find movement that makes you happy — a brisk walk, dancing, cycling or trying
something new. Let joy lead, not performance. And if your energy is low on a day you
expected it to be high: that's allowed too. Explore
[exercise in the follicular phase](/articles/exercise-follicular-phase/) and
[exercise around ovulation](/articles/exercise-around-ovulation/).

## And during the luteal phase?

In the luteal phase, just before your period, some people notice more need for calm.
Your body is doing a lot of work, and softer movement can feel good then: walking, calm
yoga, or simply moving at your own pace. Listen to the signals you get. A few deep
breaths or a gentle stretch can be enough today. Read more about
[movement in the luteal phase](/articles/exercise-luteal-phase/).

## How do I find my own rhythm?

Every cycle is different, and no description fits you perfectly. The loveliest thing you
can do is stay curious about your own patterns. When do you feel strong? When do you
long for rest? By gently tracking this, over time you discover what works for you.
Movement is allowed to feel like care for yourself, not an assignment.
