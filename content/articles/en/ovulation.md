# Ovulation

Around the middle of your cycle, ovulation takes place: an egg is released. For many
people this coincides with an energy peak and an open, social feeling. It is also the
time around your fertile window.

This is general information and not medical advice. The calendar method gives an
estimate, not certainty — if you want to avoid pregnancy, use a reliable
contraceptive method and discuss it with a doctor if needed.

## How the ovulatory phase can feel

Around ovulation, many people notice more energy, a desire for connection and a clear
feeling. It can be a pleasant moment for things that ask a bit more of you — socially
or in movement.

## The fertile window

The days around ovulation form your fertile window. Whether you are hoping for a
pregnancy or want to avoid one: knowing which phase you are in gives calm and context.
Paced shows this as statistics and patterns, never as certainty or a guarantee.

## Movement and nutrition

At your energy peak? That can be a good moment for more vigorous movement, followed by
good recovery. Fibre-rich, colourful foods — think vegetables and berries — fit this
phase nicely. No rules, just care.
