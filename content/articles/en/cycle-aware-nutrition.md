# Eating in tune with your cycle

Your needs change through your cycle, and your appetite may follow. Cycle-aware eating
does not mean being strict or counting — it means listening to what your body enjoys in
each phase. No rules, no forbidden foods, no numbers: just food as a form of care.

This is general information, not nutritional or medical advice. If you have specific
questions or dietary needs, talk to a doctor or dietitian.

## The idea behind cycle-aware eating

In one phase you may naturally crave more, or want warmth; in another, freshness and
lightness. Gently tuning your food to that often feels better — without having to
forbid or make up for anything.

## By phase, briefly

- **[During your period](/articles/what-to-eat-during-your-period/)**: iron-rich,
  warm and comforting food.
- **[Follicular phase](/articles/nutrition-follicular-phase/)**: fresh, light meals
  that suit rising energy.
- **[Around ovulation](/articles/eating-around-ovulation/)**: fibre and colourful
  vegetables at your energy peak.
- **[Luteal phase](/articles/nutrition-luteal-phase/)**: complex carbohydrates and
  comfort — eating more here is okay.

## Nutrition without diet culture

There is no "good" or "bad" food in your cycle. A craving for something savoury or
sweet is information, not a problem to solve. Cycle-aware eating is about tuning and
caring, never about punishing or making up for things.
