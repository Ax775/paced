# Exercise during your period

During your period all kinds of things shift in your body, and you may feel that in how
you feel and how you move. Some days you want to be active, other days your body mostly
asks for rest. There is no right or wrong way to handle this. In this article we calmly
describe what can happen when you move during your period, so you can choose what suits
you right now.

## Can you exercise during your period?

Moving during your period is perfectly fine — if you feel like it. There's no reason to
put yourself on pause just because you're menstruating. At the same time, you don't have
to. For some people gentle movement feels relieving with cramps or a restless feeling;
for others, standing still and resting is exactly what's needed. Both are allowed.
Movement is allowed to feel like care, not something you have to earn or make up for.

## What movement suits this phase?

In the first days of your cycle your energy is often a bit lower, and that can translate
into a preference for gentler forms of movement. Think of a calm walk, light yoga,
stretching or moving to music that makes you happy. A gentle stretch can be enough today.

Feeling energetic? Then go ahead and move more intensely. Listen to your body's signals:
tiredness, a heavy feeling or a need to pause are not signs of failure, but information.
You don't move to make something right, but because it feels good.

## Does moving help with cramps and a restless feeling?

Many people find that gentle movement can help them feel a little looser during their
period. A short walk or calm stretches can give a pleasant feeling and make space in
your head. For some this works nicely, for others not — and that's completely okay.

Notice that movement doesn't help right now, or asks more than you have? Then you may
leave it. Rest is just as valuable. A few deep breaths or lying down with a warm bottle
can be exactly enough for today.

## How do you listen to your body while exercising?

Listening to your body starts with checking in with yourself before you move. How do you
feel? Do you want to move, or does your body mostly ask for softness? While moving, you
can keep tuning in: does this still feel good, or do I want to ease off?

You don't have to follow a fixed schedule or push on when it no longer feels good. Your
body works hard these days, and giving yourself permission to take it calmly is not
weakness — it's listening. Some days you move a lot, other days little, and both are
valuable.
