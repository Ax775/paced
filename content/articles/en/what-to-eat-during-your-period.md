# What to eat during your period

During your period your body loses some iron, and many people notice more need for
warmth and comfort. You don't have to force or avoid anything — listen to what feels
good and give your body what it enjoys in these days.

This is general information, not medical or nutritional advice.

## Iron-rich foods

Leafy greens (spinach, kale), lentils, beans, pumpkin seeds and whole grains are
natural sources of iron. A squeeze of lemon or some pepper alongside helps your body
absorb iron from plant sources better.

## Warmth and comfort

Warm meals — soups, stews, tea — feel good to many people as the body turns inward.
There is nothing wrong with comfort food; eating is allowed to simply be pleasant.

## Listen to your appetite

More appetite during your period is normal and okay. A craving for something sweet or
savoury is not something to correct. Eat with attention to what you enjoy, not with
rules.
