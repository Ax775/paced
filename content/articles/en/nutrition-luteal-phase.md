# Nutrition in the luteal phase

In the luteal phase, the run-up to your period, your body does more work and many
people have more appetite or a need for comfort. That is normal — eating more in this
phase is okay and even logical. You don't have to make up for it or earn it.

This is general information, not medical or nutritional advice.

## Complex carbohydrates

Whole grains, oats, sweet potato and legumes give a longer, steadier feeling of
fullness. They fit nicely with the need for heartier food this phase often brings.

## Magnesium-rich foods

Dark chocolate, pumpkin seeds, nuts and leafy greens are natural sources of magnesium,
which some people say they benefit from in this phase. Enjoy them as part of your food,
not as an obligation.

## Cravings are okay

More appetite in the luteal phase is very common. Nothing here is wrong and there is
nothing to make up for — listen to your hunger and eat with attention. Warmth and
comfort are allowed to be there.
