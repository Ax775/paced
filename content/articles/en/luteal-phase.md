# The luteal phase

The luteal phase is the time between ovulation and your next period. Your body slowly
turns inward in this phase. Many people notice more need for rest, and sometimes
changes in mood, sleep or appetite. That is part of the rhythm — nothing is wrong
with you.

This is general information, not medical advice. If your symptoms weigh on you, talk
to a doctor.

## How the luteal phase can feel

In the run-up to your period you may feel more sensitive, more tired or more
irritable. More appetite or a need for comfort is normal too. It helps to adjust your
expectations: this is not a moment to ask everything of yourself.

## Rest and sleep

Sleep can be a little more restless in this phase. A calm evening routine, fewer
screens and warmth can feel good. Tired? Then you may rest — that is not failing, that
is listening to your body.

## Nourishment that comforts

Many people want heartier, warmer food in the luteal phase. Complex carbohydrates and
magnesium-rich foods (like dark chocolate or pumpkin seeds) feel supportive to some.
Eating more in this phase is okay and even logical — your body is doing more work.
