# Exercise in the follicular phase

After your period your follicular phase begins — a time when many people notice some
space and lightness slowly returning. Maybe your body feels more energetic, maybe you
have more desire to get moving. And maybe not, and that's completely fine too. In this
article we describe what can come up in this phase around movement, so you can choose
what suits you.

## What happens in your body during the follicular phase?

The follicular phase begins on the first day of your period and runs up to ovulation. In
the days after your period, oestrogen gradually rises. Many people experience a little
more energy in this period, a clearer head and a desire to take things on.

This is no law — every cycle and every day is different. See it mainly as background, not
as something that has to feel this way. Your body is not a schedule you must follow.

## What movement suits this phase?

Because many people experience more energy in this phase, it sometimes feels good to do
something more active: a brisk walk, dancing, cycling, strength training or a workout
that brings you joy. It can be a nice moment to try something new or go just a little
further than usual — purely because it feels good.

But there's no required list. Movement is allowed to feel like joy today, not
performance. Sometimes a calm stretch is enough, and that fully counts too.

## How do I know if I have enough energy?

The best gauge is you. Check in calmly before you start: does your body feel light and
inviting, or is it asking for more softness?

Energy can differ per day, even within the same phase. Maybe you start full of good
spirits and notice halfway that your body needs something else. Then you may switch.
Listening to what's there is more valuable than holding on to a plan.

## And if I still feel tired or flat?

That's just as much part of it. Not every follicular phase brings a wave of energy, and
that says nothing about you. Poor nights, busyness or simply a quiet day can all play a
role.

Resting is also valuable. A walk at a slower pace, some gentle movement, or nothing at
all — all choices are okay. You don't have to make up for anything. Your body constantly
does work you don't see, and you're allowed to give it room.

## How do I track this calmly?

Getting to know your patterns can help you look at your movement with more kindness. See
also the overview [movement in tune with your cycle](/articles/movement-and-your-cycle/)
and the explainer on the [follicular phase](/articles/follicular-phase/).

It's not about streaks or goals, but about gently noticing what your body tells you.
