# Movement in the luteal phase — staying gently in motion

The luteal phase is the time between ovulation and the start of your period. In these
days all kinds of things shift in your body, and you may notice it in your energy, your
mood or how heavy or light movement feels. There is no right or wrong way to move now —
only the way that suits you. In this article we describe what many people experience, so
you can listen more gently to your own rhythm.

## How does movement feel in the luteal phase?

In the first half of the luteal phase you often still have some energy to spare. As your
period gets closer, movement can start to feel heavier. That's not a sign something is
wrong — your body is working hard in the background, and that's allowed to take up space.

Maybe you notice you tire a bit faster, or that your body asks for a calmer pace.
Movement may then feel like care rather than performance. A walk, some light stretches or
a calm bike ride can already be enough.

## What movement suits this phase?

Many people feel good with gentler forms of movement in these days. Think of yoga,
walking, swimming or calm strength exercises where you mainly notice how it feels.

Want a more intense workout anyway? That's allowed too, if it feels good. Listen to your
body's signals: is it asking to continue, or to ease off the pace? There is no movement
you "must" do — joy and attention are a lovely guide.

## What if I have little energy?

Less energy is sometimes part of this phase. On those days rest may be just as valuable
as movement. A short walk outside, a few minutes of gently moving to music, or simply
standing still and breathing — it all counts.

You don't have to make up for anything. Rest is also something your body carries. Give
yourself permission to take it easier when needed; there's nothing wrong with that.

## How do I stay in touch with my body?

Checking in with yourself can help you tune movement to what you need today. Ask yourself
gently: how does my body feel now? Do I feel like moving, or does it mostly ask for calm?

By tracking your patterns across several cycles, you get to know your own rhythm better.
See also the overview [movement in tune with your cycle](/articles/movement-and-your-cycle/)
and the explainer on the [luteal phase](/articles/luteal-phase/).

## A calm closing

Movement in the luteal phase is allowed to be light and inviting. There is no ideal and
no obligation — only your body, your energy and your choice, day after day.
