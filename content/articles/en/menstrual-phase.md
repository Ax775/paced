# The menstrual phase

The menstrual phase is the time when you menstruate, usually the first days of your
cycle. For many people this is a moment to turn inward: less energy, more need for
rest. That is not weakness — your body is doing hard work.

This is general information, not medical advice. If you have a lot of pain or
concerns, talk to a doctor.

## How the menstrual phase can feel

Tiredness, a need for rest and a quieter feeling are very common in these days.
Cramps can be part of it; warmth, like a hot water bottle or warm tea, can help. Give
yourself permission to slow down.

## Rest over performance

There is no reason to push yourself into a packed schedule or a hard workout in this
phase. Light movement, like a short walk or some stretching, is more than enough if
it feels good.

## Nourishment that supports

In the menstrual phase your body benefits from iron-rich foods, like leafy greens,
lentils or pumpkin seeds. A squeeze of lemon helps your body absorb iron from
plant sources. It is about care, not rules.
